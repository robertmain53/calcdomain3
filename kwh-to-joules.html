Formula

\[ \text{Joules} = \text{kWh} \times 3{,}600{,}000 \]

Where 1 kilowatt-hour equals exactly 3,600,000 joules (or 3.6 megajoules).

How It Works

The kilowatt-hour (kWh) is a unit of energy equal to the energy consumed by a device with a power of 1 kilowatt operating for 1 hour. Since 1 kW = 1000 W and 1 hour = 3600 seconds, 1 kWh = 1000 × 3600 = 3,600,000 joules.

Example Calculation

To convert 5 kWh to joules:

  1. Multiply 5 kWh by the conversion factor 3,600,000
  2. 5 × 3,600,000 = 18,000,000 J
  3. Result: 5 kWh = 18,000,000 J (or 18 MJ)

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a kilowatt-hour?

A kilowatt-hour (kWh) is a unit of energy equal to the energy consumed by a 1-kilowatt device operating for 1 hour. It's commonly used to measure electricity consumption on utility bills.

How much is 1 kWh in joules?

1 kWh equals exactly 3,600,000 joules (3.6 megajoules or 3.6 MJ). This is derived from 1 kW × 1 hour = 1000 W × 3600 s = 3,600,000 J.

Why do utility bills use kWh instead of joules?

Kilowatt-hours are more practical for household energy consumption because they result in manageable numbers. Using joules would require very large numbers (millions or billions) for typical household usage.

How many kWh does a typical home use?

The average US home uses about 877 kWh per month (approximately 10,524 kWh per year), though this varies significantly by location, season, and household size.

Data Source and Methodology

The conversion factor is based on the standard definition: 1 kWh = 1000 W × 3600 s = 3,600,000 J. This is an exact conversion with no rounding error.
