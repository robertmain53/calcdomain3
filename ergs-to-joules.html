All calculations are based on the conversion factor provided by authoritative scientific data sources. 1 erg is equal to 1e-7 joules. All calculations are rigorously based on this formula and data.

The Formula Explained

Glossary of Terms

  • Erg: A unit of energy in the CGS system.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

To convert 1,000,000 ergs to joules, multiply 1,000,000 by 1e-7, which equals 0.1 joules.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is an erg?

An erg is a unit of energy or work in the centimeter-gram-second (CGS) system, equal to 10^-7 joules.

Why use joules instead of ergs?

Joules are the standard SI unit of energy, making them more widely recognized and used in scientific calculations.

How does the conversion work?

The conversion is based on a simple multiplication using the conversion factor: 1 erg = 1e-7 joules.
