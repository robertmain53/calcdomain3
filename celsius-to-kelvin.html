Celsius to Kelvin Converter

Convert temperatures from Celsius to Kelvin instantly. Essential for scientific calculations, physics, chemistry, and thermodynamics applications.

Result

273.15 K

How this converter works

The conversion from Celsius to Kelvin is a simple addition. The Kelvin scale starts at absolute zero (the lowest possible temperature), which is -273.15°C. Therefore, to convert from Celsius to Kelvin, we simply add 273.15.

Formula

\( K = °C + 273.15 \)

Practical Example

To convert 25°C (typical room temperature) to Kelvin:

\( K = 25 + 273.15 = 298.15\text{ K} \)

This conversion is fundamental in scientific work, particularly in thermodynamics, gas law calculations, and chemistry.

Key Temperature Points

  • Absolute zero: -273.15°C = 0 K
  • Water freezes: 0°C = 273.15 K
  • Room temperature: ~20-25°C = ~293-298 K
  • Body temperature: 37°C = 310.15 K
  • Water boils: 100°C = 373.15 K

Frequently asked questions

What is the Kelvin scale?

The Kelvin scale is an absolute temperature scale that starts at absolute zero (0 K), the theoretical temperature at which all molecular motion ceases. It is the base unit of temperature in the International System of Units (SI) and is primarily used in scientific contexts.

Why add 273.15 to Celsius to get Kelvin?

The offset of 273.15 exists because the Kelvin scale starts at absolute zero (-273.15°C). By adding 273.15 to any Celsius temperature, we shift the reference point from water's freezing point (0°C) to absolute zero (0 K).

Is Kelvin used outside of scientific contexts?

Kelvin is primarily used in scientific fields like physics, chemistry, and engineering because it's an absolute scale with no negative values. This makes calculations involving temperature ratios and thermodynamic equations much simpler and more accurate.

Why doesn't Kelvin use the degree symbol (°)?

Unlike Celsius and Fahrenheit, the Kelvin scale doesn't use the degree symbol. We write "273 K" not "273°K". This convention emphasizes that Kelvin is an absolute scale, not relative like Celsius or Fahrenheit.

Can I convert Kelvin back to Celsius?

Yes! To convert Kelvin to Celsius, simply subtract 273.15. For example: 300 K - 273.15 = 26.85°C.
