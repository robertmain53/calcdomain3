Data Source and Methodology

All calculations are based on the methodology outlined in Porter's competitive forces model. For more information, see Porter's Five Forces Analysis Guide.

The Formula Explained

Total Score = Supplier Power + Buyer Power + Threat of New Entrants + Threat of Substitutes + Industry Rivalry

Glossary of Terms

  • Bargaining Power of Suppliers: The ability of suppliers to drive up prices.
  • Bargaining Power of Buyers: The power of customers to drive prices down.
  • Threat of New Entrants: The ease with which new competitors can enter the market.
  • Threat of Substitutes: The likelihood of customers finding a different way of doing what you do.
  • Industry Rivalry: The intensity of competitive pressure in the industry.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is Porter's Five Forces Analysis?

It is a framework for analyzing the level of competition within an industry.

How can this tool help me?

It assists in understanding the competitive forces at play and devising strategies for improvement.

Can I use this tool for any industry?

Yes, the model is applicable across various industries to gauge competition.

What do the scores mean?

Higher scores indicate greater competitive forces, which may require strategic planning to tackle.
