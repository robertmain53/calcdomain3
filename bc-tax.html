The Formula Explained

The provincial tax is calculated using the formula:

\( \text{Provincial Tax} = \text{Income} \times \text{Tax Rate} \)

Glossary of Terms

  • Income: Your total annual earnings before any deductions.
  • Tax Rate: The percentage of income that is paid as tax to the provincial government.
  • Provincial Tax: The amount of money paid to the provincial government based on your income.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Consider an individual with an annual income of $50,000. Using a hypothetical tax rate of 5%, the provincial tax would be calculated as follows:

\( \text{Provincial Tax} = 50,000 \times 0.05 = 2,500 \)

The final income after paying provincial tax would be $47,500.
