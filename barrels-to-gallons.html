Formula

The formula to convert barrels to US gallons is:

\[ \text{Gallons} = \text{Barrels} \times 42 \]

One barrel (petroleum barrel) is equal to 42 US gallons. This is the standard conversion used in the oil and gas industry.

Data Source

All calculations are based on the standard petroleum barrel conversion where 1 barrel (bbl) = 42 US gallons. This conversion is defined by the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) and is widely used in the petroleum industry.

Glossary

Barrel (bbl)
A unit of volume used primarily in the petroleum industry. One petroleum barrel equals 42 US gallons. Note that beer and wine barrels have different volumes.
US Gallon (gal)
A unit of volume in the US customary system equal to 128 US fluid ounces or approximately 3.785 liters. Used for measuring liquids like gasoline, milk, and water.
Petroleum Barrel
The standard barrel used in the oil industry, equal to 42 US gallons. Established as a standard in the early days of the petroleum industry in Pennsylvania.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a barrel?

A barrel is a unit of volume commonly used for measuring liquids such as oil and beer. The petroleum barrel, used for crude oil, equals 42 US gallons.

How many gallons are in a barrel?

There are 42 US gallons in one petroleum barrel. However, beer barrels contain 31 gallons and wine barrels may vary.

Why convert barrels to gallons?

Converting barrels to gallons is useful for understanding and measuring liquid volumes in more common units, especially when comparing quantities or calculating costs.

Is this tool accurate?

Yes, this tool uses the standard conversion rate of 42 gallons per petroleum barrel based on NIST standards. Results are displayed to two decimal places.

Can I use this tool for other liquid types?

This tool converts petroleum barrels. If you're measuring beer or wine, note that those barrels have different volumes (31 gallons for beer, varying for wine).
