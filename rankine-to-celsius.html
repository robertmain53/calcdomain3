The Formula Explained

Conversion Formula: °C = (°R - 491.67) × 5/9

Glossary of Terms

  • Rankine (°R): A scale of absolute temperature using Fahrenheit degrees.
  • Celsius (°C): A scale and unit of measurement for temperature where 0 °C is the freezing point of water.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

To convert 540 °R to Celsius, apply the formula: °C = (540 - 491.67) × 5/9 = 26.85 °C.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the Rankine scale?

The Rankine scale is an absolute temperature scale that uses Fahrenheit degrees. It is used primarily in engineering systems where heat computations are performed using degrees Fahrenheit.

Why convert Rankine to Celsius?

Converting Rankine to Celsius is essential for scientific calculations and for understanding temperature in a scale that is widely used in most of the world.
