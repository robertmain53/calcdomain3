Formula

The formula to convert BTU to Joules is:

\[ \text{Joules} = \text{BTU} \times 1055.06 \]

One British Thermal Unit (BTU) is equal to 1055.06 Joules. This conversion is based on the International Table (IT) BTU definition.

Glossary

BTU (British Thermal Unit)
A unit of heat energy defined as the amount of heat required to raise the temperature of one pound of water by one degree Fahrenheit. Commonly used in heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC) systems.
Joule (J)
The SI derived unit of energy, work, or amount of heat. One joule equals the energy transferred when applying a force of one newton through a distance of one meter.
International Table BTU
The most commonly used definition of BTU, equal to exactly 1055.05585262 joules. Often rounded to 1055.06 J for practical calculations.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you convert BTU to Joules?

To convert BTUs to Joules, multiply the number of BTUs by 1055.06. This gives you the equivalent energy in Joules.

What is a BTU?

A British Thermal Unit (BTU) is a measure of thermal energy. It's the amount of heat needed to raise one pound of water by one degree Fahrenheit at sea level.

Why is this conversion important?

This conversion is crucial for energy calculations in fields such as HVAC, engineering, and environmental science. It allows professionals to work with both imperial and SI units.

Are there different types of BTUs?

Yes, there are several BTU definitions (ISO, IT, thermochemical). This calculator uses the International Table (IT) BTU, which is the most common standard.

Can I convert Joules back to BTU?

Yes, divide the Joules value by 1055.06 to convert back to BTU. For example, 10,550.6 J ÷ 1055.06 = 10 BTU.
