Data Source and Methodology

All calculations are based on the standard conversion of 1 terabyte (TB) being equal to 1024 gigabytes (GB). Source: Wikipedia

The Formula Explained

Terabytes (TB) = Gigabytes (GB) / 1024

Glossary of Terms

  • Gigabyte (GB): A unit of data storage equal to 1,024 megabytes.
  • Terabyte (TB): A unit of data storage equal to 1,024 gigabytes.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

If you have 2048 GB of data, dividing by 1024 gives you 2 TB.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How do you convert from gigabytes to terabytes?

To convert gigabytes to terabytes, divide the number of gigabytes by 1024.

Why use 1024 for conversion?

Data storage is calculated using binary, where each step is a power of 2. Hence, 1024 (2^10) is used instead of 1000.
