Data Source and Methodology

All calculations are based on the Compton scattering formula derived from quantum mechanics principles.

The Formula Explained

The change in wavelength (Δλ) is given by the formula:

Δλ = (h / (m_e * c)) * (1 - cos θ)

Where h is Planck's constant, m_e is the electron rest mass, c is the speed of light, and θ is the scattering angle.

Glossary of Terms

  • Initial Wavelength: The wavelength of the photon before scattering.
  • Scattering Angle: The angle at which the photon is scattered.
  • Scattered Wavelength: The wavelength of the photon after scattering.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is Compton scattering?

Compton scattering is a quantum mechanical phenomenon where X-rays or gamma rays are scattered by electrons, resulting in a change in wavelength.
